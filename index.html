Powerlifting Statistics

Powerlifting is a competitive strength sport that focuses on three primary exercises: the squat, bench press, and deadlift. Athletes in powerlifting aim to lift as much weight as possible in these three movements. It's a test of pure strength and determination, with competitors divided into various weight classes to ensure fairness. The total weight lifted by a powerlifter is the sum of their best squat, bench press, and deadlift attempts. This total, often referred to as the "total kg".

Furthermore, there are different styles of powerlifting such as raw and multi-ply. Raw powerlifting involves lifting with minimal gear, typically just a singlet, belt, knee sleeves or wraps, and wrist wraps. This category emphasizes the lifter's natural strength and form, making it a purer test of raw power. In contrast, multi-ply powerlifting allows for the use of specialized supportive gear like squat suits, bench shirts, and deadlift suits, which provide additional assistance in lifting heavier weights.
